A bit about me

Almost all my career has been in IT project management, where I collaborated with cross functional teams to deliver products that meet requirements. But that came to a head in 2016, which was around the time I started toying with the idea of making a career change into software engineering. Though I've always had a thing for building software, I didn't really pay it much attention, let alone take the necessary steps to make it into a career.

My journey started by first understanding HTML and CSS. Webflow was my go to place for that, and it was that platform that helped solidify my understanding of the nuances of CSS as I was able to build and style many websites just for fun. Then I participated in a Google + Andela initiative which gave me access to Pluralsight courses where I honed my ES5 and ES6 skills. By 2019 and after taking a full stack course on Udemy, I was building a real estate web app - Bricalot, using the MEN stack.

I finally decided it was time to make the career change, and that led me to General Assembly's immersive course in software engineering which I believe has effectively prepared me to contribute meaningfully to your codebase, and to help your organistaion inch closer to her engineering and product goals.
